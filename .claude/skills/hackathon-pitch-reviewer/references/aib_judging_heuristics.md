# AI BEAVERS Judging Heuristics

What `deck_structure_scorer.py` encodes, in prose. Use to explain *why* a
criterion is flagged. Verbatim from the published judging guide
(`docs/AI BEAVERS founder hackathon judging and scoring guide.md`).

## The rubric (6 criteria, weights = 100)

- **Problem + customer clarity (20)** — names a specific customer/user,
  explains the pain clearly, avoids vague "everyone" markets.
- **Market + business potential (20)** — opportunity plausible, buyer /
  distribution path makes sense, could become a real company. Bottom-up sizing.
- **Product execution + demo (20)** — built something concrete during the
  hackathon; works enough to demonstrate the core idea, even if unpolished.
- **AI-native leverage + technical approach (15)** — AI as a meaningful
  capability, not decoration; technical choices support the product; team can
  explain what was built.
- **Evidence, insight + founder edge (15)** — real insight: customer knowledge,
  domain expertise, early validation, relevant data, or a strong reason they
  are the right people.
- **Pitch clarity (10)** — the 3-min pitch is clear, direct, judge-friendly;
  problem → product → evidence → next step, no buzzwords.

## Scoring scale (how raw scores map)

90-100 exceptional · 70-89 strong · 50-69 solid · 30-49 early/unclear ·
1-29 not competitive. Our gate target ≥ 80 leaves margin for content polish.

## What counts as evidence (criterion 5 — the most-missed)

Real enough to change how judges see the project: a specific customer segment;
a conversation/quote/pain point from the target market; domain knowledge from
the team's own work/network/lived experience; a small competitor scan; a
working demo of the core workflow; a repo with same-day commit history; a clear
next step (who you'd test with, what you'd charge).

**Weak evidence:** "the market is huge" with no customer detail; "everyone needs
this"; generic AI-trend slides; a demo that looks good but doesn't prove the
core use case; old work presented as new.

## How judging runs on the day

- Submit by 19:00: public repo + ≤7-slide deck (live URL optional).
- Preliminary: every team pitches 3 min, no Q&A, in an assigned room.
- ~6-7 finalists → 3 min pitch + 2 min Q&A. Winners after.
- Judges may inspect GitHub history and ask questions if something looks off.

## Structural mistakes the scorer catches

- More than 7 slides (hard cap — disqualifying structure).
- No specific customer ("everyone" / "für alle").
- Top-down market ("1% of $100B") instead of bottom-up.
- "No competition" claim.
- Adjective-only traction instead of numbers / a real quote.
- Generic AI-trend language ("AI-powered", "revolution") with no real capability.
- Missing one of the 7 core work-slides.

> One idea per slide. Numbers, not adjectives. The demo proves the core
> workflow; the deck introduces; the 3-minute spoken pitch wins it.
